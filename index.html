Project Titan-Mycelium

Genetically Directed Carnivorous Mycelium for Self-Healing Structural Materials

Architecture Overview

Project Titan-Mycelium outlines the biological redesign of Pleurotus ostreatus (Oyster Mushroom). By modifying target genetic pathways, the organism transitions from a standard scavenger into an active, predatory structural matrix capable of defending, reinforcing, and repairing engineered partitions.

Engineering Specifications

1. Pathway Swap: Pest Sensing

Native fruit-sensing proteins are entirely replaced. The modified PO-X1 lineage expresses high-density pest-odorant and constricting receptors along the hyphae, locking the fungal grid into an active, permanent hunting mode optimized for boring insects.

2. Micro-Weaponry: Chitinase Secretion

Upon constriction feedback, the modified loops deploy an aggressive, localized payload. A high-density Chitinase & Protease Cocktail is secreted directly at the point of contact, degrading insect exoskeletons for rapid nutrient absorption.

3. Composite Material Strength

Upregulation of cell-wall synthesis pathways forces hyper-sclerotization. As shown in performance benchmarks, the interlocking cellular matrix scales well past baseline fungal properties, matching the load capacities of structural concrete equivalents.

4. Autonomous Self-Healing

A full architectural integration. When structural panels encounter a breach or fracture event, localized feedback loops guide rapid cellular migration to fill the structural gap, sealing vulnerabilities autonomously.
