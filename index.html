OVERVIEW

Project Titan-Mycelium is a synthetic biology initiative aimed at engineering a living, predatory biomaterial capable of active structural defense and autonomous self-healing. By utilizing the fast-growing matrix of Pleurotus ostreatus, we have developed an organic composite that hunts structural pests while generating military-grade compressive strength.

GENETIC MODIFICATIONS

  • Constitutive Predatory Activation: Swapped native nutrient-sensing promoters with an aggressive, continuous promoter loop. The fungal hyphae are permanently locked into hunting mode, bypassing normal nutrient scarcity triggers.
  • Chitin-Sclerotin Hyper-Expression: Upregulated the metabolic pathways responsible for cell-wall synthesis. The resulting mycelial mats interlock with extreme density, forming a lightweight composite stronger than standard concrete.
  • Targeted Enzymatic Payload: Engineered localized secretion of high-density chitinase and protease cocktails directly at the hyphal loop tips, melting pest exoskeletons on structural contact.
