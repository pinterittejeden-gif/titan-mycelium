Project Titan-Mycelium

Filamentous Macro-Cord Networks for Self-Healing Structural Armor

Architecture Overview

Project Titan-Mycelium outlines the biological redesign of filamentous fungal matrices. Rather than relying on micro-scale predatory traps, the engineered lineage synthesizes thick macro-scale cords designed to envelop structural invaders, utilizing contact-activated chemical degradation and an immune cellular shield to achieve a high-efficiency self-healing loop.

Engineering Specifications

1. Pathway Swap: Rhizomorph Aggregation

Native sensory proteins are replaced with specialized pest-odorant and mechanoreceptors. When boring insects breach a panel, the network triggers localized mass-migration, weaving fine hyphae into thick, rope-like mycelial cords that mechanically enmesh the pest.

2. Weaponry: Chitosan Shading & Secretion

To avoid self-digestion, the strain expresses chitin deacetylase, converting its outer cell walls into immune chitosan. Upon insect contact, apical tips flood the site with target-specific chitinase, liquefying the pest's cuticle while leaving the fungal structure perfectly intact.

3. Composite Material Strength

Upregulation of cell-wall cross-linking vectors forces hyper-sclerotization during the initial growth phase. As shown in performance benchmarks, the continuous filamentous brick achieves compressive thresholds matching structural concrete equivalents.

4. Metabolic ROI & Self-Healing

The enzyme loop operates at a net-positive energy return. Absorbed nutrients from digested pests fuel rapid, localized hyphal growth directly inside fracture zones, autonomously sealing structural compromises up to 95.2% baseline strength.
